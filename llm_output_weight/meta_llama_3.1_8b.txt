=== Problem 26 ===
Prompt:
A gold bar weighs 1.2 kg. After removing 150g from it and adding 350g of silver, the total weight becomes 1.4 kg. What is the density ratio of gold to silver by weight?
Response:
Initial weight of gold = 1.2 kg
Weight removed = 150 g = 0.15 kg (converting grams to kilograms)
Weight added = 350 g = 0.35 kg (converting grams to kilograms)

Final weight after removing and adding = 1.4 kg

Weight of gold left = Initial weight - Weight removed 
= 1.2 kg - 0.15 kg
= 1.05 kg

Since the final weight is 1.4 kg, the weight of silver added can be calculated as:
Weight of silver = Final weight - Weight of gold left
= 1.4 kg - 1.05 kg
= 0.35 kg

Density ratio of gold to silver by weight = (Weight of gold) / (Weight of silver)
= 1.05 kg / 0.35 kg
= 3 

So, the density ratio of gold to silver by weight is 3
